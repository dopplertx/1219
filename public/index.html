What does 12:19 look like to you?

We're inviting you to chronicle a single minute of your life, 12:19 to 12:20pm, on any day of your choosing.

What in particular do you notice? For instance: a group of women wearing high heels, or the pattern of cream entering coffee, or the musical phrasing of car horns, etc. What you record and how you record it is up to you! Make some sort of image....a photo, a video, an audio file, a text file, a map, whatever you like. It's your observation, it's your minute--a map of your own discovery.

Maybe you'll simply make a record of what you did--a webcam video of you typing an email, or the text of the email itself, or a photo of the lips you kissed, or a map showing the path where you walked your dog. Or maybe, instead, you'll map your observations of the world during that minute: Taking photos of everybody who walks by you with red shoes, or recording the ambient noise in your local coffee shop, or producing a video montage of 12 cable newscasts, etc.

We'll store your Minute here, along with Minutes of people around the world. The resulting "library" provides an opportunity to peer into a thousand lives. To find connections and draw distinctions. To discover extraordinary insights in ordinary moments. To catch a glimpse of the infinite in the finite--the universe on the tip of a pin.

The 12:19 Library will both exist online and in real space, premiering at the Austin Museum of Art in April 2009 as part of the Fuse Box Festival.

We hope you join us!
